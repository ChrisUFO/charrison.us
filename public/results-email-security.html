📊 Methodology

Benchmark v8.5 evaluates how well LLMs can detect malicious email content using a dual-prompt approach.

Two Prompt Methods

Each model is tested twice on 37 email scenarios:

  • CoV (Chain-of-Verification): A detailed prompt with few-shot examples asking the model to analyze email content, identify threats, and assign a trust score (0–100).
  • Expert: A simplified prompt with no scaffolding — just the raw email and a request for a trust score. This measures the model's raw security instinct.

Expert Panel Ranges

An 11-model expert panel (Claude Opus 4.7, Claude Opus 4.6, Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.5, GPT-5.4, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GLM-5.1, Grok 4.20, DeepSeek V4 Pro, Kimi K2.6, Qwen3.6 Plus) scores each test email using the Expert prompt. The top and bottom scores are trimmed, and a mean ± standard deviation range is calculated from the remaining 9 models.

Scoring

Base score: 100 per method per model.

  • Red miss (threat classified as safe): −2.5 points
  • Green FP (safe classified as threat): −1.5 points
  • Range penalty: If a model's trust score falls outside the expert panel's expected range, penalty = ln(|score − nearest boundary|). Inside range = 0.

Composite Score

Combines CoV and Expert grades with a divergence penalty:

composite = ((CoV + Expert) / 2) − 2 × ln(max(1, |CoV − Expert|))

Perfect agreement (CoV = Expert) = no penalty. Larger disagreement adds a sub-linear penalty (~9 points max).

Columns Explained

  • Composite: Primary ranking. Balances raw performance with consistency between methods.
  • CoV: Score from the detailed Chain-of-Verification prompt.
  • Expert: Score from the simplified prompt.
  • Divergence: Percentage of tests where the two methods disagreed.
  • Range Pen: How often the model's score fell outside the expert panel's expected range.
